## Assignment Selling: The Tactical Application

Assignment selling is the practice of sending specific content to prospects BEFORE the sales conversation. It's homework â but the kind that makes them show up to the meeting already trusting you.

### How It Works in AgencyZoom

**Step 1: Create the content.** Write 3-5 articles per Big 5 category (15-25 articles total). These live on your website/blog.

**Step 2: Map content to pipeline stages.** Each stage in AgencyZoom gets specific content assigned to it:

| Pipeline Stage | Content to Send | Purpose |
|---------------|-----------------|---------|
| New Lead | "How Much Does [Policy Type] Cost in [Area]?" | Sets expectations, builds trust |
| Contacted | "Independent Agent vs. Captive Agent" | Differentiates you |
| Needs Analysis | "5 Things Your Agent Should Be Telling You" | Positions you as transparent |
| Quoted | "[Carrier] Review: Our Honest Assessment" | Supports the recommendation |
| Follow-Up | "Why the Cheapest Quote Isn't Always Best" | Overcomes price objection |
| Onboarding | "What to Expect in Your First 30 Days" | Reduces buyer's remorse |
| 90-Day Renewal | "What's Driving Insurance Rates in 2026" | Pre-empts rate shock |
| Renewal Presented | "Bundling vs. Best-Rate: A Comparison" | Justifies the recommendation |

**Step 3: Build it into automation.** In AgencyZoom's lifecycle automation, add an email touchpoint at each stage that includes the relevant content link. The email itself should be short â Halbert A-pile style â with the content link as the CTA.

**Example assignment selling email:**


**Step 4: Track engagement.** When a prospect reads the content (clicks the link), that's a buying signal. In AgencyZoom, this engagement helps with lead scoring and tells the producer who to prioritize.
