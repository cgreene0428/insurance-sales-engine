## The 7-Part Narrative Structure

The core insight: **Your customer is the hero. Your agency is the guide.** Every piece of copy, every email, every text message follows this narrative structure. This is the messaging DNA of the Trust and Grow Framework.

## The 7 Elements

### 1. Character (The Hero = Your Customer)

The hero wants ONE clear thing. In P&C insurance, common hero desires by line:

**Personal Lines:**
- Homeowner: "I want to protect my family and everything we've built"
- Auto: "I want to drive with confidence knowing I'm covered if something happens"
- Umbrella: "I want to make sure one bad day doesn't wipe us out"

**Commercial Lines:**
- Small business owner: "I want to focus on growing my business without worrying about what could go wrong"
- Contractor: "I need coverage that doesn't slow down my ability to bid and win jobs"
- Restaurant owner: "I want to protect the business I've poured my life into"

Keep it to ONE desire per audience segment. If you confuse, you lose.

### 2. Problem (The Villain)

Every story needs a villain. In insurance, the villain is NOT a person â it's a force of frustration. Problems have three layers:

**External Problem** (the surface issue):
- "I don't have the right coverage"
- "My premium keeps going up"
- "I don't understand my policy"

**Internal Problem** (how it makes them FEEL):
- "I feel confused and overwhelmed by insurance"
- "I'm worried I'm overpaying but don't know how to tell"
- "I feel stupid when agents throw jargon at me"
- "I'm afraid I'll find out I'm underinsured when it's too late"

**Philosophical Problem** (why it's just WRONG):
- "People shouldn't have to be insurance experts to get proper protection"
- "Families deserve an advocate who fights for them, not a salesperson"
- "Business owners shouldn't lose sleep over whether their insurance actually covers them"

The internal problem is the most powerful in copy. When your email names the feeling the prospect is having RIGHT NOW, they feel heard â they know you're the expert, and they know they're in the right place.

### 3. Guide (Your Agency)

Two qualities make a guide:

**Empathy**: "We know what it's like to stare at a policy and have no idea if it's actually protecting you."

**Authority**: Demonstrate through:
- Years of experience ("We've helped 2,000+ families in [Area]")
- Specific expertise ("Our team has 47 combined years in P&C insurance")
- Testimonials (social proof from similar clients)
- Carrier relationships ("We work with 20+ top-rated carriers")
- Results ("Our clients save an average of $437/year")

Never lead with authority alone. Empathy first, then authority.

### 4. Plan (Make It Simple)

Always present a 3-step plan. The prospect's brain needs to see a clear, easy path:

**Example for Personal Lines:**
1. **Tell us your story** â Quick call or form, 10 minutes
2. **We shop for you** â We compare rates across 20+ carriers
3. **You choose with confidence** â We walk you through options in plain English

**Example for Commercial Lines:**
1. **Share your situation** â Tell us about your business
2. **We build your safety net** â Custom coverage, no gaps
3. **Focus on what you do best** â We handle the insurance so you don't have to

### 5. Call to Action

**Direct CTA**: "Get Your Free Quote" / "Schedule Your Coverage Review" / "Call Us Today"
- Make it a button. Make it obvious. Repeat it everywhere.

**Transitional CTA** (Lead Magnet / Assignment Selling piece):
- "Download: The P&C Coverage Checklist Every Homeowner Needs"
- "Free Guide: 5 Questions to Ask Before You Renew Your Policy"
- "Watch: What Your Agent Isn't Telling You About Your Deductible"

The transitional CTA is where the Trust and Grow content engine lives â Big 5 articles and assignment selling pieces that build trust before the sales conversation.

### 6. Failure (Stakes)

Paint a specific picture of what happens without action:
- "A single uncovered claim could cost your family $50,000+"
- "Every month without proper coverage is a gamble with your family's future"
- "47% of small business owners who experience a major claim without adequate insurance close within 2 years"

Use failure sparingly but specifically. Vague fear doesn't work. Specific, real scenarios do.

### 7. Success (The Transformation)

Show the after-state â how life is different with your agency:
- "Imagine knowing â with absolute certainty â that your family is protected"
- "Picture opening your renewal notice and feeling confident, not confused"
- "What would it feel like to have an insurance advisor you actually trust?"

Three transformation types:
1. **Status**: "Be the homeowner who made the smart choice"
2. **Completeness**: "Finally feel like you have a real partner looking out for you"
3. **Self-realization**: "You deserve to feel in control of your financial protection"

When building out an agency's AgencyZoom, fill in this template first:

```
PLAN:
  Step 1: [Simple first step]
  Step 2: [What the agency does]
  Step 3: [The outcome for the customer]

CALL TO ACTION:
  Direct: [Primary CTA]
  Transitional: [Lead magnet / content offer]

FAILURE: If you don't act, [specific negative consequence]

SUCCESS: With us, you'll [transformation description]
```

Create one BrandScript per major audience segment (homeowner, auto, commercial, life/health if applicable). Use the personal lines BrandScript as the primary one for general messaging.
